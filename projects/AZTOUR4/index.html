Facts

Arizona became the 48th state on February 14, 1912.

The saguaro cactus blossom is the official state flower. The white flower blooms on the tips of the saguaro cactus during May and June. The saguaro is the largest American cactus.

The Palo verde is the official state tree. Its name means green stick and it blooms a brilliant yellow-gold in April or May.

Attractions

    • Tombstone ×

    At its peak, Tombstone is said to have been the fastest growing city between St.Louis and San Francisco.There were over one hundered saloons, numerous restaurants, a large red-light district, schools, churches, newspapers, and one of the first public swimming pools (which is still used today). Tombstone's post office was established December 2, 1878 and has yet to be discontinued.The most famous event in Tombstone's history was the famed Gunfight at Ok Corral, which didn't actually happen at the corral, but in a vacant lot on Fremont Street. On October 26, 1881, members of the "Cowboys", had a run-in with Wyatt, Virgil and Morgan Earp with help from Wyatt's friend Doc Holliday. 24seconds and 30 shots later, Billy Clanton, Tom and Frank McLaury were mortally wounded. In many people's opinion it was this one event that has kept Tombstone alive for all these years.There was actually five Earps. They were Virgil, Morgan, Wyatt, Warren and James. There were two newspapers on Tombstone, The Tombstone Epitaph edited by Jonh Clum and The Tombstone Daily Nugget founded by H.M Woods in 1880. The Epitaph took a Republican view on events in town while The Nugget was supported by the Democrats. There was an ice-cream parlour that was very popular with the locals, including Wyatt Earp. There was a few theaters in town, the most famouos of them being Schieffelin Hall and The Bird Cage Theater. Sirens were used in Tombstone to mark the begining of a new shift for the miners. After the gunfight on October 26, 1881, sirens went off from the Vizna mine and miners poured up onto the street, armed and ready. Saloons were open 24 hours a day in Tombstone. This included The Oriental, The Crystal Place Saloon, The Eagle Saloon and The Alhambra. Saloons could be quite decadent and served Whiskey and Brandy, a six-year-old Kentucky Apple Brandy, Gin Rum, Sherry, Port, English Ale, Scotch, Millers Extra and 26 different imported wines. The Oriental bragged at having piano and violin concerts every night and was llit with chandeliers. Restaurants in Tombstone such as The Can Can,The Russ House, The Elite House ant The Malson Doree, catered to all tastes including fine dining. Fresh shrimp and oysters were brought in from California to Tucson in registered train cars, then transported down to Tombstone.

    • Grand Canyon ×

    Arizona is home of the Grand Canyon National Park. The state's most popular natural wonders include the Grand Canyon, Havasu Canyon, Grand Canyon Caves, Lake Powell/Rainbow Bridge, Petrified Forest/Painted Desert, Monument Valley, Sunset Crater, Meteor Crater, Sedona Oak Creek Canyon, Salt River Canyon, Suprerstion Mountains, Picacho Peak State Park, Chiricahua National Monument, and the Colorado River. Grand Cnayon's Flaming Gorge got its name for its blazing red and orange colored, twelve-hundred-foot-high walls. Grand Canyon's Disater Falls named to commemorate the site of a previous explorer's wreck. Grand Canyon's Marble Canyon got its name from its thousand-foot-thick seam of marble and for its walls eroded to a polished glass finish.

    • Colossal Caves ×

    Beautiful 2,400 acre desert park, on the National Register of Historic Places, showcasing the crystal-filled Colossal Cave and historic La Posta Quemada Ranch, cave tours, mueseum, western trail rides and wooded picnic areas and spectacular cowboy cookouts under the stars. Bonecrusher and Fang and the names of some of the largest stalactites in Colossal Caves in Vail, Arizona. The cave was formed during the Mississippian Period, over 320 million years ago, when water seeped through the rocks and and dissolved away part of the limestone, leaving behind the third largest cave in the world. Colossal Cave is one of the 5% of the dry/dormat caves in the world, meaning that it is still alive but no longer growing. This also means it is one of the rare caves pictures can be taken in.The cave isn't the only thing that this park holds for visitors, though. A few minutes down the road, 120-year-old La Posta Quemada Ranch, site of an old stagecoach station, hostess the Ranch Headquarters House, which is home for the mueseum, research library, another beautiful gift shop, and open-air cafe serving Mexican food. Here you can sit in the natural habitat butterfly garden and watch butterflies whiz around your head and observe tortise as they wonder about their stone enclosure. There is a riding stable where trail rides are available and the Analemmatic Sundial where you can be the shadow that tells the time. Special reservations can be made for cattle drives, cowboy cookouts and kids' birthday parties. If you like to camp, there are secluded campsites near Posta Quemada Wash. While there you can hike on the 2,577 acres the Park covers and spot many animals, such as a ringtail, a coatimundi, javelina and striped skunk. Colossal Cave Mountain Park offers a variety of activities for all interest, whether you want a day-outting or rough it on a camping trip. No reservation is required for a guided cave tour, and the maximum wait is 30 minutes. There is no food, drink or gum allowed in the cave, but bottled water is permitted. Reservations are suggested for trail rides.

    • Kartchner Caverns ×

    The Caverns are located in Whetsone Mountains near Benson, Az which is 46.7 miles east of Tucson. The natural wonder is a must-see for anyone who wants a truly unique experience. The formations, flora, fauna, and geology of Kartchner Caverns are all extremely fascinating. The Caverns were discovered in 1984 by Gary Tenen and Randy Tutfs. However, the public didn't learn about the Caverns until 1988 when Gary and Randy told the owners James and Lois Kartchner. Kartchner Caverns are home of the world's largest Stalactites. These are known as soda straw stalactites averaging twenty-one feet three inches long. It's also home to the most massive and tallest column in Arizona. It's called the Kubla Khan and is 58 feet tall.Guided tours only. You usually have to have reservations: (520) 586-2283. You have to pick up your tickets at least an hour prior to your tour or they become open for grabs.This is an informative tour of a living cave. The visitors center is one of the best. Excellent exhibits.There are several tours you can enjoy. The Rotunda/Throne tour, Helmet Tour, Headlamp Tour and The Big Room Tour. The Big Room Tour is only open October - April. From April on, it is left to accommodate the myotis bats which fly in to use it as a nursery. Children under 7 are not allowed on the Big Room Tour.

    • Chiricahua National Monument ×

    Chiricahua National Monument was discovered in 1924 and became a National registered Historic place in 2008. The Monument is located just southeast of Wilcox, Arizona. Before it became a national monument, the Chiricahua Apache Indians called the Chiricahua Mountains home. If you enjoy hiking then you'll be in heaven here. You can download a free hiking guide on the National Park Service website.
